About Me



Hello! I'm Richard, and I use He/Him/His pronouns. I'm a 3rd year undergraduate student at Northwestern University currently pursuing a combined BS/MS in Computer Science, a double major in Statistics, as well a Kellogg Certificate in Financial Economics as part of the Kellogg Certificate Program for Undergraduates.

Academic

Broadly, I am interested in technology's potential to energize and revolutionize other fields, from the financial industry to healthcare to bioinformatics. From interning at startups at the Garage to conducting HCI research as a part of DTR (Design, Technology, Research) to leading tech consulting projects as Technical Lead (now co-President) of Northwestern's Develop + Innovate for Social Change Club (DISC), my interests in terms of CS have gradually spanned a wider range as I took more classes and gained more experience. Currently I am primarily interested in the design and development of complex, robust software systems that process and analyze high volumes of data.
